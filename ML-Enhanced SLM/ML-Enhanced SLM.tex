\begin{abstract}
This document provides a comprehensive study of \textbf{Machine Learning-Enhanced
Selected Mapping (SLM)} techniques for \textbf{Peak-to-Average Power Ratio (PAPR)
reduction in OFDM systems}.  It is designed for an undergraduate engineering
student in their final year who has already studied OFDM and classical SLM, and
now seeks to understand how modern machine learning methods---neural networks,
autoencoders, deep unfolding, and genetic algorithms---can overcome the three
fundamental limitations of classical SLM: high computational complexity, side
information overhead, and blind detection difficulty.

The document synthesizes six original research papers spanning 2016--2025,
contextualized by one comprehensive survey, covering six distinct
ML-enhanced PAPR reduction approaches: PRNet (deep autoencoder), NN-SCF
(neural signal cancellation), GA-SLM (genetic algorithm-optimized SLM),
ESLM-AE (extended SLM with autoencoder for DCO-OFDM), PR-DUN (deep
unfolding network), and DL-AE (autoencoder for coherent optical OFDM).

Each approach is presented with full architectural detail, mathematical
derivations, training procedures, simulation parameters for reproducibility,
result analysis, common misconceptions, and honest assessments of
limitations.  A comparative analysis and future directions chapter
synthesizes the findings across all approaches.
\end{abstract}
